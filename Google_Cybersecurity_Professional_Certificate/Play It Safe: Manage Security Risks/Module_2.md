## Key Concepts Learned
**Security Framewroks**- guidelines used for building plans to help mitigate risk and threats to data and privacy.
**Security Control**- sefegaurds design to reduce specific security risk.
  ##### 3 common types:
  1. **Encryption** - the process of converting data from readable format to encoded format. plaintext to ciphertext
  2. **Authentication** - the process of verify someone
  3. **Authorization** - granting access to specific resources within the system. Permission to granting access
     
The **CIA triad** is a model that helps inform how organizations consider risk when setting up systems and security policies.

##### 6 core function of NIST CTF
1. Govern - emphasize strong cybersecurity governance
2. Identify - the management of cybersecurity risk and its effect on organizations people and assets. 
3. Protect - strategy used to protect organization through implementing of policies, procedures, trainings and tools. 
4. Detect - identifying potential security incidents and improving monitoring capabilities to increase speed and detection
5. Respond - making sure that the proper procedures are used to contain, neutralizes, and analyze security incidents, and implement securty process
6. Recover - process of returning affected systems back to normal operations

**OWASP Security Principle**
- minimize attack surface area
- Principle Least Priveledge
- Defense in depth
- Seperation duties
- keep security simple
- fix security issues completely

After learning security frameworks, controls and principles, by conducting security audits is is where it works together.
**Security audits** - a review of an organization's security controls, policies, and procedures against a set of expectations. There are two types of security audit: **External & Internal**
