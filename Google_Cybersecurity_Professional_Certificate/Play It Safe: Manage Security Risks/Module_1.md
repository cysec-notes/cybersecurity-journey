# Module 1: Security Domain
## Overview
This module explains the focus of CISSP’s eight security domains. Then, primary threats, risks, and vulnerabilities to business operations are identified and defined, along with a discussion of the threats, risks, and vulnerabilities entry-level security analysts focus on most. Additionally, NIST’s Risk Management Framework (RMF) is introduced.

## Key Concepts Learned
#### What is Security posture?
An orgaization ability to manage its defense of critical assets and data, and react to change.

8 security domain of CISSP
1. **Security & Risk managemnt** - focused on defining security goals and objective, risk mitigation, compliance, business contiunity, and legal regulations.
   - risk mitigations - right procedures and rules to reduce the impact of risk
   - Business continuity - organizations ability to maintain productivity by establishing risk disaster recovery plan.
2. **Asset Security** - focused on security digital and physical assets. It's also related to storage, maintenance, retention, and destruction of data
3. **Security Architecture & Engineering** - focused on optimitzing data security by ensuring effective tools, system, processes to protect assets and data.
4. **Communication & Network Security** - focused on managing and securing physical networks and wireless communications.
5. **Identity & Access Management** - focused on acces and autorization to keep data secure, by making suer users follow established policies to control and manage assets.
   3 components of IAM: Identification, authentication, authorization, accountability
6. **Security Assesment & Testing** - focused on conducting security contol tetsing, collecting and analyzing data, and conducting security audits to monitor risk, threats, and vulnerabilites. Organizations might employ penetration testers, often referred to as “pen testers,” to find vulnerabilities that could be exploited by a threat actor. 
7. **Security Operation** - focused on conducting investigations and implement preventative measures
8. **Softaware Development Security** - focusing securing coding practices. Secure application and services

**Threat** - any circumstance or event that can negatively impact assets. Ex. Social engineering
**Risk** - is anything can impoact the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of assets.
**Vulnerability** - a weaknesses that can be exploit by a threat.

## steps in Risk Management framework
1. Prepare - related to activities that are necessary to manage security and privacy risks before a breach occurs
2. Categorize - used to develop risk management processes and tasks
3. Select -  means to choose, customize, and capture documentation of the controls that protect an organization
4. Implement - means to implement security and privacy plans for an organization
5. Assess - to determine if established controls are implemented correctly
6. Authorize - refers to being accountable for the security and privacy risks that may exist in an organization
7. Monitor - means be aware of how systems are operating
